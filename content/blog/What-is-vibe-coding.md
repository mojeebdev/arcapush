## The Definition

Vibe coding is a software development approach where builders use AI tools to create applications through natural language prompts — describing what they want in plain English instead of writing traditional code line by line.

The term was coined by **Andrej Karpathy**, OpenAI co-founder and former Tesla AI director, in a post on X in February 2025. Collins English Dictionary named it their **Word of the Year for 2025**.

The core idea: you describe the vibe of what you want to build. The AI writes the code. You iterate by describing what to change. You ship.

---

## Why It Matters

Before vibe coding, building software required years of learning syntax, frameworks, and engineering patterns. A solo founder with a great idea but no coding background had two options: learn to code (years) or hire engineers (expensive).

Vibe coding eliminates both barriers.

A non-technical founder can now build a working SaaS product in a weekend. A designer can ship their own app. A domain expert — a doctor, a lawyer, a teacher — can build the tool their industry desperately needs, without a single line of traditional code.

This is not a marginal improvement. It is a **structural shift** in who gets to build software.

## The Tools Powering Vibe Coding

The vibe coding ecosystem runs on a handful of AI-powered builders:

**Cursor** — An AI-first code editor that lets developers prompt their way through complex codebases. Valued at $29.3B, backed by Andreessen Horowitz and Accel. The tool of choice for technical vibe coders.

**Lovable** — A full-stack app builder. Describe your product, Lovable generates the entire frontend and backend. Valued at $6.6B, backed by Alphabet's CapitalG, Nvidia, and Accel.

**Replit** — A collaborative coding environment with AI assistance built in. Valued at $3B, backed by Andreessen Horowitz and Google.

**Bolt (StackBlitz)** — An in-browser full-stack builder. Instant deployment, no setup required.

**Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini** — The underlying AI models that power many of these tools and can be used directly for code generation.

## Who is a Vibe Coder?

A vibe coder is anyone who builds software primarily through AI prompting rather than traditional coding. This includes:

- **Solo founders** who move from idea to product without a technical co-founder
- **Designers** who can now ship their own prototypes and products
- **Domain experts** building niche tools for their industries
- **Traditional developers** who use AI to dramatically accelerate their output
- **Entrepreneurs** who test business ideas by building MVPs in days, not months

The defining characteristic is not the absence of technical knowledge — many vibe coders are experienced engineers. It is the **AI-first workflow**: prompt, iterate, ship.

## The Criticism

Vibe coding has its skeptics. The main concerns:

**Code quality** — AI-generated code can be insecure, inefficient, or brittle. Products built on vibe coding may work at small scale but fail under load or scrutiny.

**Maintainability** — If a founder cannot read the code their AI wrote, debugging becomes dependent on AI assistance indefinitely.

**Security** — Vibe-coded applications may have security vulnerabilities their founders cannot identify or fix.

These are legitimate concerns. The best vibe coders treat AI-generated code as a starting point — reviewing, testing, and refining rather than shipping blindly.
