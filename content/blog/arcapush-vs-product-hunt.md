# Arcapush Is Not the Next Product Hunt. That's the Point.

*By Mojeeb Adewale — Founder, Arcapush.com | [@mojeebeth](https://x.com/mojeebeth)*

---

Every time a founder discovers Arcapush, the same question comes up.

*"Is this like Product Hunt?"*

No. And the difference is not subtle.

---

## The Misconception

Product Hunt is a daily launch platform. You submit your product, it goes live at midnight, you hustle for upvotes for 24 hours, and then it gets buried under tomorrow's launches. The entire model is built around a single day of momentum.

Arcapush is an encyclopedia. Your startup does not get a launch day. It gets a **permanent page** — indexed by Google, discoverable by anyone searching for vibe coding tools, AI-native products, or the specific problem you solve. There is no leaderboard. There is no voting. There is no expiry date.

That distinction changes everything about how you should think about it.

---

## What Product Hunt Is Good At

Product Hunt is genuinely great at one thing: generating a spike. If you have an audience already, if you have built a launch list, if you can mobilize your network on day one, Product Hunt will give you a visible number to point to. That spike has real value for early user acquisition and community momentum.

But a spike is not a foundation. The day after your launch you are no longer on the front page. The day after that you are not even on the second page. Your Product Hunt page still exists, but nobody is finding it organically. It becomes a trophy, not a discovery channel.

---

## What Arcapush Is Actually For

Arcapush exists for a different moment — not the launch day, but **every day after it**.

When a VC is researching the AI tools space at 11pm, they are not scrolling Product Hunt's archive. They are searching. "AI productivity tools built with Cursor." "Vibe coding startups." "Lovable alternatives." If your product is in Arcapush's [registry](/registry) with a clean page, a clear problem statement, and the right category — you are findable in that moment.

That is the discovery that actually leads somewhere.

This is also why Arcapush is free for every vibe coding founder to submit. No funding required. No credentials check. If you built something real with AI tools — Cursor, Lovable, Bolt, Replit — your product belongs in the registry. The only barrier: you have to have built something.

---

## The Goal Is Not to List Funded Startups

Here is where most people get it backwards.

Arcapush is not a directory of startups that already have VC money. It is a discovery layer that helps vibe coding founders **get noticed by** the VCs who have it. The listing comes first. The backing can follow.

A founder who built a Cursor-powered analytics tool submits to Arcapush for free. Their product gets a permanent indexed page. An investor browsing the vibe coding space finds it three months later. That conversation happens because the product was findable — not because it already had a term sheet.

Product Hunt cannot do that for you. A 24-hour launch window does not serve a founder who is heads-down building and not ready to run a launch campaign. Arcapush does not require a campaign. It just requires a [submission](/submit).

---

## Every Listing Requires a Problem Statement

This is the detail that matters most and gets the least attention.

Every Arcapush listing requires a **problem statement** — a clear, specific description of the real-world problem your product solves. Not your tagline. Not your marketing copy. The actual problem.

This is why Arcapush gives VCs investor-grade signal instead of noise. Anyone can write a tagline. Not everyone can articulate, clearly and specifically, what problem they are solving and why their product solves it better.

That requirement filters for founders who have thought it through. And it's exactly what investors are looking for when they're sourcing early-stage deals.

---

## Use Both. Understand What Each One Does.

The right move for most vibe coding founders is not Arcapush *or* Product Hunt. It is both — used for what they are actually good at.

Launch on Product Hunt for the spike and the community moment. Submit to Arcapush for permanent discoverability. Product Hunt gets you attention from early adopters on day one. Arcapush gets you found by an investor who was not paying attention on your launch day.

One is a moment. The other is infrastructure.

If you built something real with AI tools, your product deserves to be findable permanently — not just for 24 hours.
