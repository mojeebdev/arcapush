## The Official Recognition

In late 2025, Collins English Dictionary announced **vibe coding** as their Word of the Year for 2025.

Collins defines vibe coding as:

> *The practice of using artificial intelligence to write computer code by describing in natural language what one wants the program to do.*

This is a significant moment. Collins' Word of the Year selection is not a popularity contest — it is an editorial judgment about which new word or phrase best captures a meaningful cultural or societal shift in the year it emerged. Previous winners have included words that defined their eras.

Vibe coding earned the distinction because it captured something genuinely new: a fundamental change in who gets to build software and how.

---

## How a Tweet Became a Dictionary Entry

The term originated in a single post by **Andrej Karpathy** on February 2, 2025. Karpathy — co-founder of OpenAI, former Director of AI at Tesla — described a new workflow he had adopted: using AI tools to build software by describing intent in natural language rather than writing code manually.

He called it vibe coding.

The post spread rapidly. Within weeks it had been covered by every major technology publication. Within months it had entered common usage in startup communities, VC pitch decks, and developer forums. By the end of 2025, Collins had enough linguistic evidence of widespread use to justify a dictionary entry and a Word of the Year award.

The journey from tweet to Word of the Year took approximately ten months.

---

## Why This Selection Matters

Collins selects words that reflect genuine cultural shifts — not just technology trends. The selection of vibe coding signals that the practice has moved beyond the technology industry into broader awareness.

When a software development methodology earns mainstream cultural recognition, it means:

**Non-technical people are building software.** Collins tracks language as it is actually used. Vibe coding entered common usage partly because people outside the traditional developer community were adopting it and talking about it.

**The barriers to software creation have genuinely changed.** The word captures not just a tool or a technique, but a shift in the relationship between people and technology. That is the kind of change dictionary editors notice.

**The ecosystem reached critical mass.** By the time Collins made their selection, billions of dollars in VC investment had flowed into vibe coding tools. Millions of people were using them. The cultural footprint was real.

---

## The Full Collins Definition in Context

Collins defines vibe coding as a practice, not a tool. This is precise. Vibe coding is not Lovable or Cursor or Replit — those are tools. Vibe coding is the approach: describing in natural language, letting AI implement, iterating on the result.

The definition is deliberately technology-agnostic. It does not specify which AI tools are used. This reflects how language actually works — the word describes a practice that can be done with many different tools, and will continue to be relevant as those tools evolve.

## What Comes After Word of the Year

Being named Word of the Year by Collins means vibe coding now has a formal place in the English language. It will appear in Collins dictionaries. It is a citable, authoritative term.

For founders building vibe coding products, this has practical implications:

**Press coverage becomes easier.** Journalists can now reference vibe coding without explaining what it is. The term has mainstream legitimacy.

**Investor conversations become cleaner.** A VC who might not follow developer tools closely now has cultural context for what vibe coding is and why it matters.

**Wikipedia and academic citation.** A Collins dictionary entry is considered a reliable source for Wikipedia purposes. The formal definition gives writers and editors a citable reference for the term's meaning and origin.
